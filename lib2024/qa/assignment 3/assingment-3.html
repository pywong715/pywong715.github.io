The Best Bookstores in Hong Kong

Hong Kong's top bookstores offer more than just books, with activities such as book dating, mini-concerts, tea-tasting, and running classes. Books serve as a medium for connecting community members who share a love for reading. By bonding with store owners and customers, you can have a memorable experience in independent bookstores in Hong Kong.

Check out our list of the best Hong Kong bookstores.

Tsuen Wan

Jat1 Pui4 Books

Inspired by the Japanese single bookstore, Morioka Shoten Ginza, the founder of Jat1 Pui4 Books incorporated the concept of "A Single Room, A Single Book" into their local practice.

Jat1 Pui4 turns the books they sell into exhibitions, providing readers with the opportunity to immerse themselves in the book and create their own unique reading experiences. The current exhibition, which is focused on Wei-jing Lee's The Necessary of Old-Fashioned Dating, is curated in the bookstore, and features a walking date based on the book for readers to participate in.

Kwun Tong

Mellow Out

Mellow Out combines shared space and a bookstore to create a diverse and unique experience. In addition to browsing the bookshelves, customers can explore events happening in the shared space, which range from tea-tasting and calligraphy classes to book dating.

Mellow Out also offers regular running classes led by a professional trainer, providing an opportunity for regulars to connect with each other. This bookstore not only offers intellectual nourishment but also opportunities for physical exercise.

Fortress Hill

Traces of books

Traces of Books is a self-funded library run by social worker Map Tang. Upon entering, visitors will find a cozy space with two walls of books and cushions on the wooden floor. Despite the lack of financial resources and employees, the library offers free access to its collection. Visitors are encouraged to explore on their own, as there are no librarians, check-out services, or catalogues.

However, Tang showcases her weekly favorite books on top of the bookshelf by the entrance as starting points for regulars, affectionately referred to as "tracers," to begin their reading journeys.

Yuen Long

Homecoming

Homecoming is a unique community space that not only serves as a bookstore but also promotes local agriculture. By offering workshops on food tasting, tea tasting, and farming, Homecoming encourages readers to connect with the natural environment and local food production.

Although currently closed for business, Homecoming continues to organize events to explore the beauty of Kam Tin and educate visitors on the importance of supporting local agriculture and natural resources. Join the community and discover the value of locally grown products.
